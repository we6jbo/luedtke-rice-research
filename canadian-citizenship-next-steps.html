Current Canadian Citizenship Context

Canada changed its citizenship-by-descent rules through Bill C-3, which took effect on December 15, 2025. The change removed the first-generation limit in some situations. This means some people who were previously blocked by older rules may now be able to apply for proof of citizenship.

The official proof document is a Canadian citizenship certificate. If a person wants to find out for sure whether they are a Canadian citizen, the person can apply for a citizenship certificate and have Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada, also called IRCC, review the case.

Important caution: Canadian citizenship law can depend on birth dates, birthplaces, parentage, prior citizenship status, naturalization history, and the law in effect at different times. This page is research guidance, not legal advice.
